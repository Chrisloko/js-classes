Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to exercise and diet to lose weight?

No. However, when patients implement a healthy diet and lifestyle the weight loss happens faster and in a greater amount.

Will I gain more weight when I stop taking Semaglutide?

No. Semaglutide medication will not change your genetic predisposition to burn calories and gain weight. Therefore, the patient must develop during the months of treatment good eating and exercise routines to maintain the desired weight. In addition, some patients may take a couple of months off and get back to taking Semaglutide to reach their ideal weight.

What are the side effects of Semaglutide?

The two main side effects of Semaglutide are nausea and constipation. Both side effects may be caused by incorrect patient dosage. They can also be treated with Zofran or MiraLAX. Patients with medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) should avoid taking Semaglutide.

What other medications may interact with Semaglutide?

Because Semaglutide lowers blood sugar, combining it with other medications that do the same such as insulin and diabetes may be dangerous.

How does Semaglutide compare to other weight loss medications?

One of the reasons Semaglutide is getting a lot of attention from the media is the amount of weight loss compared to other medications. Typically, most medications during trials show an average of 5% to 10% of weight loss compared to 16% of Semaglutide.
