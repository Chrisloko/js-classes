Am I a Candidate for Incisionless Otoplasty?

Eighty percent (80%) of all patients with protruding ears are ideal candidates for Incisionless Otoplasty. However, the other twenty percent (20%) eligibility depends on the patient’s unique anatomy.

After the age of 6, children’s ears are fully developed and from that age forward, patients are eligible for the Incisionless Otoplasty. Psychologically, this is when children develop their self-esteem. Thus, it’s extremely important to intervene to avoid any future distress experience.

How is the Incisionless Otoplasty Performed?

The procedure is performed at our outpatient AAAAFC certified surgery center in Jupiter, Florida. It usually takes 1 hour to perform the procedure for both ears. After thoroughly sterile skin preparation, local anesthesia is delivered at strategic points. Permanent sutures are passed through the cartilage and are invisible. Thus, expediting the healing and decreasing the chances of a negative event. The body naturally metabolizes the sutures, but the reshaped ears cartilage will remain permanently. The procedure takes approximately 1 hour, and patients can be picked up or drive home.

How to Care After an Incisionless Otoplasty?

Patients will experience swelling, bruising, and tenderness after the Incisionless Otoplasty procedure. A headband is instructed to be worn for 3 days consecutive and for 30 days (nights only) after that. Even though most patients do not complain about pain, the following prescriptions are recommended: antibiotics, anti-inflammatories, and light pain meds.

It is highly recommended to purchase an airplane pillow to avoid turning while sleeping and maintain face and torso facing up. It is also recommended to use a couple of pillows to elevate the head to minimize swelling.

*Most importantly it is recommended to cleanse the ear daily with Hibiclens Antiseptic solution to avoid infection.*

What is the Recovery for Incisionless Otoplasty?

There is absolutely no downtown with Incisionless Otoplasty. However, it is recommended to avoid lifting weights, pool, or beach and wearing helmets for 5 days to have the best cosmetics outcomes. Patients can return to light work or school the day after the treatment. We encourage each patient or parent to use reasonable judgment to engage in contact sports.
